emend Mobile App

The scope of this project was ideation, research, and lo-fi prototyping towards the development of a mobile app focused on improving daily habits.

The User Problem

Professional Mon-Fri workers have a lot of competing obligations, leaving very little time or energy to focus on activities that renew and enrich.
The emend app is an encouraging friend who will help users make time for self-care, with the support of a community that is invested in each others' success.

User Surveys

We began by gathering data about how people spend their evenings through Google Surveys. We focused on potential users who had set schedules, like students and professionals. These users were particularly important to us because their obligations imposed constraints on their time.

User Persona

Our team developed a user persona, Christina Espinosa, based on our research. She represents our target user, working professionals in their 20s and 30s who have hectic schedules.

The Storyboard

In the next step, we brought Christina to life by imagining a scenario in which she would use emend. Here, I defined her goal in the first frame, then illustrated how the app helps her achieve it.

The Userflow Diagram

The scenario imagined in the storyboard gave us a springboard from which to chart an average user's decision diagram.

The primary decision facing users is whether or not to complete their chosen activity. We have chosen not to "punish" users or frame the choice not to complete as a failure - instead, users are sent encouraging messages and helpful tips from other users. Those who complete their activities have the option to send these messages to friends or to other anonymous users. This option can also motivate users to complete their goals because they get the double satisfaction of being productive AND helping others.

eMend User Journey

Our User Journey follows a successful interaction with emend. The goal of emend is to help users establish new habits in spite other temptations or shortcuts they may encounter during their evenings, so frustrations and disinterest may occur. We believe the best option to counteract this is through positive reinforcement and affirmation. It is consistent with the app's genuine mission of caring and self-improvement.

IMLS.gov Website Redesign

The scope of this project was a cosmetic and structural redesign of the Institute of Museum and Library Services website. Our deliverables include heuristic analysis, a new site map, style guide, a new navigation, full responsive homepage redesign, as well as 3 additional pages.

The User Problem

The purpose of the IMLS website is to provide American libraries and museums the resources they need to apply for federal funding. However, as our initial heuristic and usability analysis revealed, much of the necessary information is hidden within a confusing site architecture. Our redesign is focused on building clarity and trustability through a more intuitive UI for IMLS.gov

Heuristics and Usability Analysis

The primary painpoint we identified in our analysis is a lack of transparency and availability of information. Many features and key topics are buried beneath secondary navigation menus, requiring multiple clicks before users can find what they're looking for.

Another issue is that there are multiple pathways to the same information. These pages could be condensed with a more efficient site architecture.
